What?

The Toronto Public Library allows you to renew books online, which is great. However, they only email you after your books are overdue.

They are apparently working on it, but you can use this to send you reminders until then. It will check your account once a day, and if your books are coming due in the next 3 days, it will email you a warning.

Why?

It bothers me that TPL can't already do this.

Note that I'm not blaming them for anything. I have no idea what proprietary system they may be locked into using, but I've worked with enough of those to know how painful the simplest changes can be.

Then again, maybe it was just a money grab. Even then, I don't blame them. I'd rather the money come from voluntary contributions, though. So, if you find this service useful, consider donating to the TPL.

How?

For those of you who are technically inclined, tplreminder is a Python/Django app, although the core fetching and parsing stuff is just pure Python.

If you want to run this locally instead of using the public service (which keeps your library card numbers and PINs in your own datasphere), you can find the project on Github.
